Economic Pathways Instructions

Objective:

In "Economic Pathways," you're tasked with making policy decisions to guide a country through a year filled with various global events. Your choices will determine the economic outcomes of the country, impacting GDP growth, the Consumer Price Index (CPI), and unemployment rates.

Gameplay:

  • At the beginning of each round, a global event will be presented. This event has the potential to influence your country's economy.
  • Your job is to select a policy in response to the global event.
  • Based on your chosen policy, the global event, and the type of country you're managing, you'll see economic outcomes for that round.

Scoring:

  • Your primary aim is to maximize GDP growth, which will directly add to your score.
  • Strive to maintain the CPI growth rate at 2%. Deviations from this target will result in penalties, with double penalties applying for overshooting the target.
  • Similarly, work to keep the unemployment rate at 4%. Exceeding this target will lead to double penalties.
  • Bonus points can be earned if you manage to keep the CPI and unemployment rates close to their targets:
    • 1 bonus point for deviations within 0.5%.
    • 3 bonus points for deviations within 0.1%.

Example:

Imagine a "Global Tech Boom" event occurs while you're managing a "Developed" country. In response, you choose the policy "Increase Infrastructure Spending." The combination of the event, policy, and country type results in:

  • GDP growth of 5% - directly adding 5 to your score.
  • A CPI growth rate of 3%, overshooting the 2% target by 1%. This results in a 2-point penalty since overshooting 1% is double-penalized.
  • Unemployment rate at 3%, undershooting the 4% target by 1%. This results in a 1-point penalty for undershooting the target by 1%.

Your score for that round will be: 5 (GDP growth) - 2 (CPI change) - 1 (Unemployment change) = 2.00.

Tips:

The country type you select (Developed, Emerging, or Resource-rich) and the synergies between your policy choices and global events play a crucial role. Different countries respond differently to the same set of events and policies. Always anticipate the potential outcomes and make informed decisions.
